Why SEO Matters for Your Website (And How to Get It Right)

By Arubu Enoch  |  March 2025  |  7 min read

← Back to Home

You spent time and money building a website. It looks great. The content is well-written. The design is clean and professional. But when you check your traffic, almost nobody is visiting. Sound familiar? In most cases, the missing ingredient is SEO.

Search Engine Optimisation is the process of making your website easier for search engines like Google to find, understand, and recommend to the right people. Done well, it turns your website from a digital brochure that just sits there into an active tool that brings in new visitors every single day, without paying for ads.

What SEO Actually Does for Your Business

When someone searches for a product or service you offer, you want your website to appear at the top of the results. That is the core promise of SEO. But the benefits go deeper than just traffic. Here is what good SEO really delivers:

1. Consistent, Free Traffic

Unlike paid ads that stop the moment you stop paying, organic search traffic keeps flowing as long as your pages are well-optimised. A strong SEO foundation means your website works for you around the clock, pulling in potential customers even while you sleep.

2. Higher Trust and Credibility

People trust Google. When your website appears in the top results for a relevant search, it sends a signal to the user that your business is credible and worth their attention. Being visible in search builds the kind of trust that is very hard to buy through advertising alone.

3. Better User Experience

Many of the things Google rewards with higher rankings are also things that make your website more pleasant to use. Fast load times, clear headings, easy navigation, and mobile-friendly design all improve SEO and improve the experience for every visitor, regardless of how they found you.

4. More Qualified Leads

Someone searching "freelance web designer Nigeria" is already looking for exactly what I offer. SEO puts your business in front of people who are actively searching for your product or service. That is a very different kind of audience from someone who stumbles across a social media ad. The intent is already there.

5. Long-Term Return on Investment

SEO takes time to build, but the results compound over time. A website with solid SEO becomes more valuable month by month, ranking for more terms, attracting more visitors, and generating more leads. Compared to the cost of ongoing paid advertising, the long-term ROI of SEO is consistently strong.

Why Most Small Business Websites Miss Out on SEO

In my experience working with startups and small businesses, most websites are built without much thought given to SEO. The designer focuses on how the site looks. The business owner focuses on getting the content right. And SEO falls through the cracks entirely.

Common issues I see include:

  • No title tags or meta descriptions on pages
  • Images that are too large, slowing the site down significantly
  • No clear heading structure (H1, H2, H3) for search engines to follow
  • Pages with no internal links connecting them together
  • A site that is not mobile-friendly, which Google penalises in rankings
  • No Google Search Console setup, meaning the business has no visibility into how the site is performing

None of these are difficult to fix. They just require someone who knows what to look for and how to address each issue systematically.

What Good SEO Looks Like in Practice

SEO covers a lot of ground, but for most small business websites, the highest-impact work falls into a few clear categories:

On-Page Optimisation

This covers everything on the page itself: the title tag, meta description, heading structure, keyword placement in the content, image alt text, and internal linking between pages. Getting these right tells Google exactly what each page is about and makes it far more likely to rank well.

Technical SEO

This is about the technical health of your site. How fast does it load? Is it indexed by Google correctly? Is the site structure logical? Does it work properly on mobile? Technical issues can quietly prevent even well-written pages from ranking, so fixing them is always a priority.

Local SEO

If your business serves a specific location, local SEO is particularly valuable. This includes setting up and optimising your Google Business Profile, getting listed in relevant directories, and making sure your website includes consistent location information. For businesses in Nigeria, local SEO can be a significant competitive advantage because many local businesses have not invested in it at all.

Content Strategy

Creating content that answers the questions your potential customers are actually searching for is one of the most powerful long-term SEO strategies. Blog posts, service pages, and guides that target specific search queries can bring in highly relevant traffic month after month.
