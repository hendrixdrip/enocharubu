Why Your Business Needs a Website, Not Just Social Media

By Arubu Enoch  |  March 2025  |  6 min read

← Back to Home

You have an Instagram page. Maybe a Facebook page too. You post regularly, your followers are growing, and people are engaging with your content. So why would you need a website on top of all that?

It is a question I hear a lot, especially from small business owners and startups who have built a decent social media presence and are wondering whether a website is worth the extra investment. The honest answer is yes, and the reasons go deeper than most people realise.

You Do Not Own Your Social Media Pages

This is the most important thing to understand. When you build your business on Instagram or Facebook, you are building on land you do not own. The platform owns it. And platforms change their rules, their algorithms, and sometimes their entire business model without asking you first.

We have seen this happen many times. A platform updates its algorithm and suddenly organic reach drops by 70%. An account gets flagged and suspended with no warning. A platform falls out of fashion and its users move elsewhere almost overnight. Businesses that had built everything on that platform had to start from scratch.

A website is yours. Your domain, your content, your customer data, your brand. Nobody can take it away from you, change how it works, or penalise it because of a policy update you had no say in.

People Search Google, Not Instagram

When someone in your area wants a product or service you offer, they go to Google and type it in. They do not open Instagram and search for it. That means if you do not have a website, you are completely invisible to that entire category of potential customer.

Think about how you find businesses yourself. You probably Google restaurants, contractors, services, and shops before you visit or call them. Most people do. A website, especially one with good SEO, puts your business in front of people at the exact moment they are looking for what you offer. Social media simply cannot do that.

A Website Makes Your Business Look More Credible

Right or wrong, people judge businesses by their online presence. A business with a well-designed website looks more established, more professional, and more trustworthy than one with only a social media page. Customers expect it, especially when they are making a purchasing decision.

A website also gives you space to show your full story. Your services, your pricing, your portfolio, your testimonials, your process. A social media page is limited in how much of that it can communicate effectively. A website gives you complete control over how you present yourself.

Social Media and a Website Work Better Together

Having a website does not mean abandoning social media. The two work very well together. Your social media presence builds awareness and keeps your audience engaged. Your website converts that interest into real business outcomes: inquiries, bookings, purchases, and leads.

Every social media post can link back to your website. Your website can feature your latest posts. Your contact form captures leads that a DM in an inbox might lose. The two channels reinforce each other and together are far more powerful than either one alone.

What Makes a Website Actually Work for Your Business

Not every website does its job well. A site that looks outdated, loads slowly, or is hard to navigate on a phone can actually hurt your credibility more than having no site at all. Here is what a good business website needs:

  • A clean, professional design that reflects your brand
  • Fast loading on both mobile and desktop
  • Clear information about who you are, what you do, and how to reach you
  • A contact form or booking system that makes it easy to take action
  • Basic SEO so that Google can find and rank your pages
  • A design that works beautifully on phones, since most visitors will use one

What It Actually Costs

One of the biggest reasons small businesses delay getting a website is the assumption that it is expensive. It does not have to be. A clean, professional website that does everything it needs to do is well within reach for most small businesses and startups. And the return on that investment, in the form of new customers who found you through Google and decided to trust you based on what they saw, tends to pay for itself quickly.

If you are ready to stop relying entirely on platforms you do not control, I would love to help you build something that truly represents your business and works hard to bring in new customers every day.
